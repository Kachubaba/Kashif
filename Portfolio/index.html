How it's built

Every render shares the same bones.

A consistent pipeline, so a client knows what to expect from kickoff to final delivery.

Modeling
Autodesk Maya
Built to true scale from plans, elevations, or a designer's sketch.
Lighting & Render

About

I think of a render the way a designer thinks of a room: nothing in it should be unintentional.

I'm Muhammad Kashif, a 3D visualization artist working with interior designers and studios who need a space to feel real before it exists. My job sits right between architecture and photography — I build the geometry, but what I'm really after is the way light actually falls across a linen sofa at 4pm, or how a quiet kitchen reads in flat overcast light.

Most projects start the same way: a floor plan, a few reference photos, and a conversation about mood. From there I model, light, and texture each room until it stops looking like a render and starts looking like somewhere you could sit down.
